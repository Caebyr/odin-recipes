Lasagna

Description

Lasagna, also known as lasagne, is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made in very wide, flat sheets. The same-named Italian dish is made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as ragù, béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses, and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • Cheese

    • A combination of ricotta and other cheeses, like mozzarella and Parmigiano-Reggiano

  • Tomato Sauce

    • A red meat sauce made with ingredients like Italian sausage, canned crushed tomatoes, red wine, heavy cream, herbs, and vegetables.

  • Eggs

    • Binds the ricotta mixture together and makes the cheese layer creamier

  • Meat

    • Lean ground beef or Italian sausage adds flavor and richness

  • Vegetables

    • Onion, garlic, carrots, and celery contribute flavor to the sauce

  • Herbs

    • Thyme and bay leaves add aromatic flavors to the sauce

Steps

  1. Make the sauce: You can use a meat sauce, tomato sauce, or store-bought pasta sauce. You can add ingredients like onions, garlic, celery, carrots, pancetta, beef, and pork. Season with salt and taste as you go.
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Prepare the cheese: You can use ricotta, mozzarella, or an Italian cheese blend.
  4. Assemble the lasagna: A common layering order is sauce, pasta, cheese, and repeat. You can add a layer of sauce to the pan to prevent the noodles from sticking
  5. Bake: Bake until the noodles are tender but still al dente, then continue baking until the lasagna is bubbly and golden brown.
  6. Let it rest: Before cutting, allow the lasagna to rest.
